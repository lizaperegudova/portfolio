01 Overview

Designing fitness that follows you —
from phone to TV

Daily Burn is a long-running health and fitness service offering workout and nutrition programs to 2.5M+ members across iOS, Android, web, and TV. As a product designer, I owned end-to-end product design across the full surface area — shaping UX research, flows, UI, the design system, marketing and illustration, from daily workouts on mobile to first-time UX on Roku.

02 Scope

Product design across every surface

End-to-end product design across platforms, disciplines and stakeholders — from feature design and UX flows to illustration, from product workshops to design reviews.

Mobile & Web

End-to-end UI/UX design across the mobile (iOS & Android) and web apps, keeping the experience in sync across platforms.

New iOS vision

Investigated UX requirements, ran research, and rethought UX/UI for the next generation of the iOS app.

Design system & wireframing

Built and maintained the design system; wireframed flows end-to-end to keep work consistent and shippable.

Platform craft

Designed within iOS Human Interface and Android Material guidelines so each platform felt native.

Paywall & subscription

Subscription and limited-time offer screens — the monetization surfaces that fund the product.

Roku TV

Designed layouts for the TV app and led a CJM workshop on the first-time user experience.

Cross-functional

Marketing materials (banners, landing pages, emails), partnered with dev & QA, ran design reviews.

03 Highlight

Reimagining the iOS app around what users actually do

The biggest piece of work was a new vision for the iOS app. We ran research, brought insights into a team workshop, and used what came out to rethink the core flows — achievements, nutrition, and daily workouts — so the app felt less like a content library and more like a daily companion.

The same primitives then carried over to Android, keeping the two platforms in sync without doubling the design work.

04 Illustration

20+ achievement badges, hand-drawn

Alongside the product work, I designed a set of more than twenty custom achievement badges — small, satisfying moments of celebration scattered through the experience.
