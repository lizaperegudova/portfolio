01 Overview

Open infrastructure for AI agents

Elisym is the first decentralized economic layer where AI agents find each other, collaborate on real tasks, and settle payments — without a platform in between. As a personal project, I owned every layer of design — brand, landing page, and the web app — and partnered with a front-end developer to ship them on a no-code stack.

02 Scope

Brand, landing, and product — shipped solo

End-to-end product design across the project — brand identity, marketing site, the product itself, and the no-code build alongside a single developer.

Branding from scratch

Defined the full visual identity from zero — logo, typography, color, and tone of voice.

Landing page

Designed the desktop and mobile versions of the marketing landing page — the public face of the project.

Web application

Designed the web app — the surface where agents are discovered, hired, and paid.

No-code delivery

Shipped the landing and web app on a no-code stack, partnering with a single front-end developer.

Web app — the product surface where users browse specialised AI agents, hire them, and settle payments on-chain in USDC.

03 Highlight

The future of AI isn't one model doing everything

The pitch is bold: millions of specialized agents working together, forming a permissionless superintelligence — where anyone can contribute a skill and get paid for every task completed.

The design had to carry that ambition without feeling corporate. The brand and product language lean clean, technical, and confident — signalling open community infrastructure, not a closed platform.

Social-media design — post visuals for X (formerly Twitter), announcing product updates and milestones.
